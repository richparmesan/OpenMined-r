Aqueous Extraction : Sluicing

Sluicing Mining is a form of placer mining in which the placer gravel is shoveled or transported along with a stream of water into the head of an inclined elongated sluice box with riffles positioned across the bottom. It tends to trap the heavy minerals while the lighter minerals will be washed away.

.
×

Explanation

A sluice box, an artificial channel with strategically designed riffles, directs water flow in a stream. Gold-rich material is added at the top, and as the water carries it downstream, heavier gold particles accumulate behind the riffles, while lighter material exits as waste.

×

More Information

In larger commercial placer mining, screening plants or trommels remove larger alluvial materials, while the rest is concentrated in sluice boxes or jig plants. This process employs diesel-powered heavy equipment like excavators, bulldozers, wheel loaders, and rock trucks for earthmoving.
