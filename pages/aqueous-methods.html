Aqueous Extraction : Placer Mining

Placer Mining is the process of extraction of placer deposits. Placer deposits are concentrations of heavy minerals, usually within loose alluvium that can easily be excavated and washed. Placer deposits are concentrations of heavy minerals, usually within loose alluvium that can easily be excavated and washed. Placer mining is used to exploit loosely consolidated deposits like common sand and gravel or gravels containing gold, tin, diamonds, platinum, titanium, or coal. A placer deposits is concentration of a loose minerals that has been redeposited in unconsolidated form by the action of a water like gold, tin, platinum, diamond, ruby, opal ilmenite, chromite, magnetite, phosphate, and sand and gravel
